HYPERTENSION CLINICAL PRACTICE GUIDELINES 2024
ACC/AHA Joint Guidelines — Synthetic Reference Document

1. DEFINITION AND CLASSIFICATION

Blood pressure (BP) categories for adults (≥18 years):
- Normal: Systolic <120 mmHg AND Diastolic <80 mmHg
- Elevated: Systolic 120-129 mmHg AND Diastolic <80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension (HTN): Systolic 130-139 mmHg OR Diastolic 80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: Systolic ≥140 mmHg OR Diastolic ≥90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: Systolic >180 mmHg AND/OR Diastolic >120 mmHg

2. DIAGNOSIS

Diagnosis requires confirmation on ≥2 occasions. Use validated, calibrated devices.
Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring (ABPM) is the gold standard for confirming white-coat hypertension.

3. CARDIOVASCULAR RISK ASSESSMENT

Use the Pooled Cohort Equations to estimate 10-year ASCVD risk:
- Low risk (<10%): lifestyle modification first
- High risk (≥10%): pharmacotherapy with lifestyle modification

4. TREATMENT

4.1 Lifestyle Modifications (ALL stages)
- Weight reduction: 1 mmHg per 1 kg body weight lost
- DASH diet: reduces BP 8-14 mmHg
- Sodium restriction (<1500 mg/day): reduces BP 5-6 mmHg
- Physical activity (150 min/week moderate aerobic): reduces BP 4-9 mmHg
- Limit alcohol: men ≤2 drinks/day, women ≤1 drink/day
- Smoking cessation: reduces overall cardiovascular risk

4.2 Pharmacotherapy

First-line agents (evidence A):
1. Thiazide or thiazide-like diuretics (chlorthalidone preferred over HCTZ)
2. ACE inhibitors (ACEi) — e.g., lisinopril, enalapril
3. Angiotensin Receptor Blockers (ARBs) — e.g., losartan, valsartan
4. Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs) — dihydropyridine e.g., amlodipine

Special populations:
- CKD with proteinuria: ACEi or ARB preferred (renoprotective)
- Diabetes: ACEi or ARB preferred
- Heart failure with reduced EF (HFrEF): ACEi/ARB + beta-blocker + MRA
- Post-MI: beta-blocker + ACEi/ARB
- Black patients (without CKD/HF): thiazide or CCB preferred
- Pregnancy: methyldopa, labetalol, nifedipine (ACEi/ARB CONTRAINDICATED)

Compelling indications for specific drug classes:
- Coronary artery disease (CAD): beta-blockers, ACEi
- Atrial fibrillation (rate control): beta-blockers, non-dihydropyridine CCBs
- Resistant hypertension: add spironolactone (if eGFR adequate)

4.3 BP Targets
- General adults: <130/80 mmHg
- Adults with CKD: <130/80 mmHg
- Adults ≥65 years (community-dwelling): <130 mmHg systolic
- Pregnant: 120-160/80-105 mmHg

5. MONITORING

After initiating therapy: reassess in 1 month.
At goal: every 3-6 months.
Monitor: serum electrolytes, creatinine/eGFR with ACEi/ARB initiation.

6. HYPERTENSIVE URGENCY vs EMERGENCY

Urgency: BP >180/120 mmHg, no target organ damage — oral agents, reduce over 24-48h
Emergency: BP >180/120 mmHg WITH target organ damage (hypertensive encephalopathy, ACS,
pulmonary edema, eclampsia) — IV agents, reduce MAP by max 25% in first hour.

7. SECONDARY HYPERTENSION

Suspect if: age <30, resistant to ≥3 agents, associated symptoms.
Causes: primary hyperaldosteronism, renovascular disease, pheochromocytoma, obstructive sleep apnea, thyroid disease.

References: 2017 ACC/AHA/AAPA/ABC/ACPM/AGS/APhA/ASH/ASPC/NMA/PCNA Guidelines, updated 2024.
