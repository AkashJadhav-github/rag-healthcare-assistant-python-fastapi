DIABETES MELLITUS MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL — ADA STANDARDS OF CARE 2024
Synthetic Reference Document for Healthcare Knowledge Base

1. DIAGNOSIS

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) diagnostic criteria (any one):
- Fasting plasma glucose (FPG) ≥126 mg/dL (after ≥8h fast)
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL during 75g OGTT
- HbA1c ≥6.5%
- Random plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL with symptoms

Prediabetes:
- Impaired fasting glucose: FPG 100-125 mg/dL
- Impaired glucose tolerance: 2h glucose 140-199 mg/dL
- HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

2. GLYCEMIC TARGETS

For most non-pregnant adults with T2DM:
- HbA1c: <7.0%
- Fasting glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
- Postprandial (2h): <180 mg/dL

Less stringent (HbA1c <8%): elderly, limited life expectancy, hypoglycemia unawareness, long-standing DM with poor control.
More stringent (HbA1c <6.5%): younger patients, short duration, no CVD, no significant hypoglycemia.

3. PHARMACOTHERAPY

3.1 First-line: Metformin
- Start: 500 mg once daily with meals, titrate to 1000 mg BID
- Mechanism: reduces hepatic glucose production (AMPK activation)
- Contraindications: eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m², IV contrast (hold 48h), hepatic impairment
- Caution: eGFR 30-45 (reduce dose), increased lactic acidosis risk
- Benefits: weight neutral/modest loss, low hypoglycemia risk, low cost, CV neutral/benefit
- GI side effects: nausea, diarrhea (use extended-release to mitigate)
- Vitamin B12 deficiency: monitor periodically

3.2 Second-line Agents (add to metformin or use if metformin contraindicated)

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (GLP-1 RAs):
- Agents: semaglutide (Ozempic), liraglutide (Victoza), dulaglutide (Trulicity), exenatide
- Benefits: significant HbA1c reduction, weight loss (3-5 kg), CV benefit (ASCVD, HF)
- Side effects: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (transient), pancreatitis risk (rare)
- Contraindicated: personal/family history of MEN2 or medullary thyroid carcinoma
- Preferred in: obesity, established CVD or high CV risk

SGLT-2 Inhibitors:
- Agents: empagliflozin (Jardiance), dapagliflozin (Farxiga), canagliflozin (Invokana)
- Benefits: HbA1c reduction, weight loss (2-3 kg), BP reduction, heart failure benefit, CKD protection
- Side effects: genital mycotic infections, DKA (euglycemic), volume depletion, Fournier gangrene (rare)
- Preferred in: HFrEF, CKD (eGFR ≥20), established CVD

DPP-4 Inhibitors:
- Agents: sitagliptin (Januvia), saxagliptin (Onglyza), linagliptin (Tradjenta)
- Benefits: modest HbA1c reduction, weight neutral, low hypoglycemia
- Caution: saxagliptin associated with HF hospitalization
- Well tolerated; useful in elderly

Sulfonylureas:
- Agents: glipizide, glimepiride, glyburide
- Benefits: effective, inexpensive
- Risks: hypoglycemia (highest risk among oral agents), weight gain
- Avoid glyburide in elderly (long half-life)

Insulin (when oral agents insufficient):
- Start with basal insulin: glargine (Lantus/Basaglar), detemir (Levemir), degludec (Tresiba)
- Starting dose: 10 units or 0.1-0.2 units/kg/day
- Titrate: increase by 2 units every 3 days if FPG >130 mg/dL
- Add prandial (bolus) insulin if HbA1c remains elevated despite adequate basal

4. COMPLICATIONS SCREENING

Annual:
- Nephropathy: urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR), eGFR
- Retinopathy: dilated funduscopic exam
- Neuropathy: monofilament test, vibration, ankle reflexes

Annual (established CVD) or q2y otherwise:
- Lipid panel, TSH (T1DM/T2DM)

5. HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT

Mild-moderate (conscious): 15g fast-acting carbohydrates, recheck in 15 min (15-15 rule)
Severe (altered consciousness): glucagon 0.5-1 mg IM/SC/IN or IV dextrose

6. CARDIOVASCULAR RISK REDUCTION

Statin therapy: ALL T2DM patients 40-75 years → high-intensity statin
Aspirin: only if established ASCVD (10-year risk benefit outweighs bleeding risk)
ACE inhibitor/ARB: if proteinuria, hypertension, or CKD

References: ADA Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes 2024. Diabetes Care 47 (Supplement 1).
