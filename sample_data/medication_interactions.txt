1. WARFARIN (Coumadin) INTERACTIONS

Warfarin is a vitamin K antagonist anticoagulant with narrow therapeutic index (INR 2.0-3.0).
Monitoring: INR must be checked regularly. Any new medication requires closer INR monitoring.

INCREASES warfarin effect (elevated INR, bleeding risk):
- Antibiotics: metronidazole, fluoroquinolones, macrolides (azithromycin, clarithromycin), fluconazole, trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole
- NSAIDs (all): increase bleeding risk by inhibiting platelet function AND some increase INR
- Amiodarone: potent inhibitor of warfarin metabolism (CYP2C9); can increase INR dramatically
- Statins: simvastatin, lovastatin, fluvastatin (CYP2C9 inhibition)
- Omeprazole/PPIs: modest INR elevation
- Thyroid hormone (levothyroxine): increases metabolism of clotting factors
- SSRIs: serotonin-mediated platelet dysfunction; increased bleeding
- Alcohol (acute): inhibits warfarin metabolism

DECREASES warfarin effect (subtherapeutic INR, clot risk):
- Rifampin: potent CYP inducer; dramatically reduces warfarin levels
- Carbamazepine, phenytoin, phenobarbital: CYP inducers
- St. John's Wort: herbal CYP inducer (counsel patients)
- Vitamin K-rich foods (kale, spinach, broccoli): counsel on dietary consistency
- Cholestyramine: reduces absorption

2. ACE INHIBITORS / ANGIOTENSIN RECEPTOR BLOCKERS (ARBs) INTERACTIONS

ACEi + ARB + direct renin inhibitor: TRIPLE BLOCKADE — contraindicated (hypotension, AKI, hyperkalemia)

HYPERKALEMIA combinations (monitor potassium):
- ACEi/ARB + potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone, amiloride, triamterene)
- ACEi/ARB + potassium supplements
- ACEi/ARB + NSAIDs (also reduces antihypertensive effect and causes AKI)
- ACEi/ARB + trimethoprim (acts as potassium-sparing agent)

HYPOTENSION combinations:
- ACEi/ARB + diuretics (especially volume-depleted patients)
- ACEi/ARB + alpha-blockers, PDE-5 inhibitors (sildenafil)
- ACEi initiation: first-dose effect more pronounced if patient on diuretics

3. STATINS (HMG-CoA REDUCTASE INHIBITORS)

MYOPATHY RISK — increases with these combinations:
- Simvastatin/lovastatin + strong CYP3A4 inhibitors: clarithromycin, itraconazole, HIV protease inhibitors → rhabdomyolysis risk → avoid or use alternative statin
- Simvastatin ≤10mg if combined with amiodarone, amlodipine, ranolazine
- All statins + fibrates (especially gemfibrozil): myopathy risk; use fenofibrate if needed
- Statins + cyclosporine: avoid simvastatin/lovastatin (use rosuvastatin at low dose)
- Statins + niacin: myopathy risk (high-dose niacin)

NOTE: Rosuvastatin and pravastatin have fewer CYP3A4 interactions (safer with macrolides, azole antifungals).

4. METFORMIN INTERACTIONS

- IV iodinated contrast: hold metformin 48h before and after contrast in at-risk patients (eGFR <60 or IV contrast bolus) — risk of contrast-induced nephropathy → lactic acidosis
- Alcohol: increased lactic acidosis risk
- Cimetidine: increases metformin levels (reduce dose)
- Topiramate, zonisamide: may precipitate lactic acidosis (carbonic anhydrase inhibition)

5. ANTIBIOTICS — KEY INTERACTIONS

Fluoroquinolones (ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin):
- Warfarin: increased INR
- Antacids/calcium/iron: chelation reduces absorption (take 2h apart)
- QT prolongation: avoid with other QT-prolonging drugs (antipsychotics, class III antiarrhythmics, macrolides)

Macrolides (azithromycin, clarithromycin):
- QT prolongation: azithromycin has moderate QT effect; avoid in long QT syndrome
- Clarithromycin is a potent CYP3A4 inhibitor → statins, warfarin, benzodiazepines, calcium channel blockers

Metronidazole:
- Alcohol: disulfiram-like reaction (flushing, nausea, vomiting) — avoid alcohol during and 48h after
- Warfarin: significant INR elevation

6. COMMON QT-PROLONGING DRUG COMBINATIONS (AVOID)

High-risk combinations:
- Antipsychotics (haloperidol, quetiapine, ziprasidone) + macrolides/fluoroquinolones
- Methadone + any QT-prolonging drug
- Class IA antiarrhythmics (quinidine, procainamide) + Class III (amiodarone, sotalol)
- Citalopram/escitalopram at high doses + other QT-prolonging agents

Check resources: CredibleMeds.org (QTDrugs list)

7. OPIOIDS

- Opioids + benzodiazepines: FDA black-box warning; respiratory depression, death
- Opioids + CNS depressants (alcohol, muscle relaxants, gabapentinoids): additive CNS/respiratory depression
- Tramadol + SSRIs/MAOIs: serotonin syndrome risk; lower seizure threshold
